Pioneer of Indian Badminton

Prakash Padukone was the first Indian to win the prestigious All England Championships in 1980, a feat that brought Indian badminton into the global spotlight. Born in 1955 in Karnataka, Padukone started playing badminton at a young age. His rigorous training and passion for the game led him to achieve numerous accolades both in India and internationally.

In addition to the All England title, he won numerous national championships and represented India at multiple international tournaments, cementing his place as a badminton legend.

After retiring from professional play, he has been instrumental in nurturing young badminton talent in India, setting up the Prakash Padukone Badminton Academy, which has produced many top players including world champions.
