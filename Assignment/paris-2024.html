Meet Our Players and Their Olympic Experiences

Prakash Padukone

Prakash Padukone, a pioneer in Indian badminton, inspired generations with his historic win at the All England Championships in 1980.

Saina Nehwal

Saina Nehwal made history by becoming the first Indian woman to win an Olympic medal in badminton, securing bronze at London 2012.

PV Sindhu

PV Sindhu has become a household name, winning silver at Rio 2016 and gold at the BWF World Championships, showcasing her talent on the global stage.

Pullela Gopichand

Pullela Gopichand, a former player turned coach, has produced multiple Olympic medalists and is a guiding force in Indian badminton.

Kidambi Srikanth

Kidambi Srikanth made history by becoming the first Indian male to reach the finals of the World Championships, earning a silver medal in 2017.

HS Prannoy

HS Prannoy's determination and resilience have been evident as he aims to make a mark at the Paris 2024 Olympics after overcoming injuries.

Jwala Gutta

Jwala Gutta, known for her fierce playing style, has been a trailblazer in women’s doubles, earning accolades and inspiring young players.

Ashwini Ponnappa

Ashwini Ponnappa, a dynamic doubles player, has represented India in numerous international tournaments, achieving great success alongside her partners.

Satwiksairaj Rankireddy

Satwiksairaj Rankireddy is a rising star in men’s doubles, showcasing exceptional skills and aiming for glory at the Paris 2024 Olympics.

Tanvi Lad

Tanvi Lad's hard work and dedication have led her to compete on the world stage, representing India with pride in various international tournaments.
